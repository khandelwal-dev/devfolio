## What is Reverse Engineering?

**Reverse engineering** is the process of analyzing a system ‚Äî software, hardware, or even protocols ‚Äî to understand its design, architecture, or functionality. Instead of starting with the source code or blueprint, you work backwards from a finished product.

It's used across a wide range of fields: **cybersecurity**, **software development**, **malware analysis**, and even **competitive product research**.

## Why is it important?

Reverse engineering can help you:

- Discover how a program or device works internally
- Recover lost source code from compiled binaries
- Find and fix security vulnerabilities
- Understand malware behavior
- Analyze competitor software or legacy systems
